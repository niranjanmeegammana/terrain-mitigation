# terrain-mitigation-workflow
AI-assisted GIS terrain-informed landslide mitigation design using survey-derived Dem analysis
N.W. Meegammana, H. Fernando, N.P. Vishva Kumara
Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology, ShilpaSayura Foundation
Landslides and slope instability are recurring problems in the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka, particularly during prolonged rainfall where uncontrolled runoff and slope saturation increase the likelihood of failure. Many mitigation measures focus on localized structural protection without adequately addressing terrain-scale drainage behaviour. This study presents an AI-assisted GIS and terrain-informed landslide mitigation workflow developed for the Ranawana Temple slope environment in Sri Lanka using survey-derived Digital Elevation Model (DEM) analysis. The study combines field investigation, terrain interpretation, hydrological assessment, drainage engineering, gabion toe stabilization, and cut-and-fill terrain optimization within a unified engineering framework. Survey-based XYZ elevation data were processed to generate a DEM used for contour extraction, slope analysis, runoff interpretation, flow accumulation mapping, and engineering visualization. Terrain derivatives were used to identify runoff convergence zones, unstable slope sections, and erosion-prone regions. These analyses guided the design of crest interceptor drains, bench drains, toe drainage systems, and gabion protection works. Terrain reshaping through cut-and-fill optimization was further used to improve runoff behaviour and reduce excessive slope gradients. The workflow produced field-ready outputs including contour maps, drainage layouts, gabion alignments, cross-sections, cut-and-fill models, BOQs, and 3D terrain visualizations for practical mitigation planning and implementation The results show that integrated drainage-first stabilization provides more effective slope protection than isolated retaining measures. The study demonstrates the value of combining field-based engineering judgement with GIS analysis and AI-assisted computational workflows for practical landslide mitigation planning in tropical slope environments.
